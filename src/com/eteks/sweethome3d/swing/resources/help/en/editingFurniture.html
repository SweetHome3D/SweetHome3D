Sweet Home 3D Help > Editing preferences, plan and 3D view
     Editing furniture
 

You may edit the location, the size and the angle of home furniture, either with the mouse or with the Furniture > Modify... menu.
To edit pieces of furniture with the mouse, select them in the home plan or furniture list first. To select one piece in the plan, click on it. To select multiple pieces in the plan, draw a selection rectangle around them, or click on pieces while pressing shift key.
To move the selected pieces, place the mouse pointer inside the selected box of one these pieces then drag and drop them, or press the keyboard arrows.

When one piece is selected in the plan, you may also change its size, elevation or angle with one of the four indicators that appear at each corner of the selected piece.

1.

The rotation indicator shows the corner you can drag to rotate the selected piece.

2.

The elevation indicator shows the corner you can drag to change the elevation of the selected piece.

3.

The height indicator shows the corner you can drag to change the height of the selected piece.

4.

The size indicator shows the corner you can drag to change the width and the depth of the selected piece.

When the mouse pointer is upon one of these corners, it changes to indicate you can drag and drop that corner to change the matching attribute of the selected piece of furniture. While you press the mouse button, a tooltip shows the value of the edited attribute(s).
The furniture modified with the mouse is drawn simultaneously in the plan and in the 3D view.

A piece of furniture may also be edited thanks to its pane, by double-clicking on that piece in the home plan or furniture list, or by choosing Furniture > Modify... after selecting it.

In the furniture pane, you may change its name, its rotation angle, the abcissa (X) and the ordinate (Y) of its center, the elevation of its bottom from the floor, its width, its depth, its height, its color, its visibility and whether its 3D model shape should be mirrored.
If you make a piece invisible, it won't be drawn in the home plan and 3D view, but it will still appear in the furniture list to let you make it visible again later.

•

If you want to modify the attributes of a set of pieces, select these pieces and choose Furniture > Modify.... The values of the attributes that are different from one piece to another in the selected furniture set, will appear blank in the furniture pane.

•

If you want to align a set of pieces, select these pieces and choose one of the Furniture > Align menus. Furniture are aligned on the first selected piece.

•

A piece of furniture qualified as a door or a window indicates to Sweet Home 3D to automatically make holes in walls at doors or windows intersection location. To ensure this intersection is correctly computed, check that the depth of a door or a window is always larger than the thickness of the wall in which it makes a hole. This computation isn't made for the other kinds of furniture.

•

If the items selected in the plan contain walls, furniture and dimensions, all these items will be moved with a drag and drop or the keyboard arrows. If you keep the alt key pressed while you drag and drop these items, they will be duplicated.

•

By default, the angle of a piece of furniture is a multiple of 15° to help you placing it parallel to walls in your home. You may cancel this magnetism by holding the shift key pressed during the rotation of a piece, or by disabling magnetism in the preferences pane.

•

The unit displayed by default in the tooltips and furniture pane depends on your country. You may switch to Centimeter or Inch in the preferences pane.

•

Choose Plan > Zoom in or Plan > Zoom out to change the plan scale.

•

To help you place furniture, change the point of view and/or the transparency of walls in the 3D view of your home.

•

Pieces imported into furniture catalog may also be edited by double-clicking on them.
