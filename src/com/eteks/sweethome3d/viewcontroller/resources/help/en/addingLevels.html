Sweet Home 3D Help > Creating homes, walls and furniture
     Adding levels
 

Every home is created with a default level, but Sweet Home 3D is also able to manage multiple levels, with the ability for each level to be at a positive or negative elevation. To add a new level, choose Plan > Add new level from the menu. As soon as a home has more then one level, some tabs appear at the top of the plan view and let you select the level into which you want to add some items. At the end of the tabs list, appears also a tab with a + sign that you can use to create more levels if needed.

New level showing in light color walls and ceilings of the lower level

To help you find your place in an upper level, walls and ceilings of the lower level are displayed with a light color in the plan view. If the edited level is the lowest level, walls and floors of the next level will be drawn in a light color. When magnetism is active, the cursor is magnetized on these walls and rooms to let you use more easily wall or room drawing tools. If needed you may also simply copy some walls, rooms or furniture from an other level to paste them in the new level.

Each level has an elevation and other attributes that can be changed, and if the walls or some furniture added to a lower level are higher than the elevation of the selected level they will appear in full color, as the surrounding walls and the staircase and the window on the right in the next figure.

New level showing in full color the high walls and furniture of the lower level

Adding a new level has no direct effect in the 3D view, and no floor is automatically added to separate a level from an other. It's only once you start to draw new walls, new rooms or add some furniture that you will see them appear at the elevation of the new level.

•

The height and the floor thickness of a new level can be set in the preferences panel. The elevation of a new level is computed by adding the elevation of last level, its height and the floor thickness set in preferences.

•

 Once a level is created you may change its name, its elevation and heights by double-clicking on its tab, or by choosing Plan > Modify level from the menu.

•

 The tabs at the top of the plan view list levels in the order of their elevation, then in the reverse order of their height.

•

 Selecting a piece of furniture in the furniture list will select automatically the level it was added to, if this piece can't be viewed in the current level.

•

Each level in a home can have its own background image to draw walls and rooms upon it.

•

The walls and rooms of the lower level drawn with a light color are not printed or copied in the clipboard. They are here to help you find your place and draw faster the current level.
